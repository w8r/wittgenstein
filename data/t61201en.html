6.1201
That e.g. the propositions "p" and "~p" in the connexion "~p . ~p" give a tautology shows that they contradict one another. That the propositions "p  q", "p" and "q" connected together in the form "(p  q) . (p) :: (q)" give a tautology shows that q follows from p and p  q.

That "(x) . fx :: fa" is a tautology shows that fa follows from (x) . fx, etc. etc.
